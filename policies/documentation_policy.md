# Documentation Policy

**Document ID:** DOC-POL-001
**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Owner:** Grant Operations Department

---

# 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the documentation standards required for all grant applications submitted to the organization. The objective is to ensure that every application contains sufficient, accurate, and verifiable information for an informed funding decision.

Documentation review is the first stage of the grant evaluation process. An application may be delayed, routed for manual review, or rejected if the submitted documents are incomplete, inconsistent, fraudulent, or fail to satisfy the requirements defined in this policy.

Compliance with this policy does not guarantee funding approval. It only confirms that the application contains the minimum documentation necessary to continue through the review process.

---

# 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- Registered Non-Governmental Organizations (NGOs)
- Charitable Trusts
- Societies
- Section 8 Companies
- Non-profit foundations
- Community organizations legally eligible to receive grant funding

The policy applies to every grant application regardless of requested funding amount or project category.

---

# 3. Required Documents

Every application must include the following mandatory documents.

## 3.1 Grant Proposal

The proposal should clearly describe:

- Project objectives
- Community problem being addressed
- Expected outcomes
- Implementation strategy
- Timeline
- Beneficiary details
- Monitoring and evaluation approach
- Sustainability plan

The proposal should present sufficient information for reviewers to understand how grant funds will be utilized.

---

## 3.2 Registration Certificate

Applicants must provide a valid registration certificate issued by the relevant government authority.

The certificate should clearly display:

- Organization name
- Registration number
- Registration authority
- Registration date
- Legal entity type

Expired or illegible registration certificates will require manual verification.

---

## 3.3 Budget

Applicants must submit an itemized project budget.

The budget should include:

- Personnel costs
- Equipment
- Operational expenses
- Administrative expenses
- Training costs
- Travel expenses
- Monitoring costs
- Contingency allocation if applicable

Each expense should include a brief justification.

---

## 3.4 Project Timeline

Applications should include a realistic implementation schedule.

The timeline should identify:

- Project start date
- Major milestones
- Deliverables
- Expected completion date

---

## 3.5 Bank Details

Organizations must provide:

- Bank name
- Account number
- IFSC/SWIFT code where applicable
- Account holder name

The account holder should match the registered legal name of the organization.

---

## 3.6 Beneficiary Information

Applications should identify:

- Target population
- Estimated number of beneficiaries
- Geographic coverage
- Selection criteria
- Expected impact

Where personal information is included, applicants are responsible for ensuring compliance with applicable privacy laws.

---

# 4. Supporting Documents

The following documents are recommended and may improve review confidence:

- Previous annual reports
- Audited financial statements
- Tax exemption certificates
- Impact assessment reports
- Previous donor reports
- Letters of partnership
- Government approvals
- Project photographs
- Monitoring reports
- Organizational brochures

Absence of supporting documents will not automatically result in rejection but may reduce confidence during review.

---

# 5. Document Format Requirements

Documents should be submitted in PDF format whenever possible.

Scanned documents should:

- be complete
- be readable
- have adequate resolution
- contain all pages
- preserve official seals and signatures

Password-protected or encrypted documents should not be submitted unless specifically requested.

---

# 6. Naming Convention

Applicants are encouraged to use descriptive filenames.

Examples include:

- Registration_Certificate.pdf
- Project_Proposal.pdf
- Budget_2026.pdf
- Timeline.pdf
- Annual_Report_2025.pdf

Meaningful filenames reduce processing delays.

---

# 7. Consistency Verification

Information should remain consistent across all submitted documents.

Reviewers should verify consistency for:

- Organization name
- Registration number
- Bank account holder
- Address
- Authorized signatory
- Contact information
- Project title
- Requested funding amount

Conflicting information may indicate clerical errors or potential fraud and should be investigated.

---

# 8. Authenticity Verification

Submitted documents should appear genuine and complete.

Indicators supporting authenticity include:

- Official government seals
- Registration authority information
- Consistent formatting
- Digital signatures where applicable
- Matching organizational details
- Official letterheads

Applicants may be asked to provide original documents during manual verification.

---

# 9. Indicators of Potential Fraud

The following observations should increase reviewer attention:

- Missing pages
- Edited registration numbers
- Altered logos
- Inconsistent fonts
- Different organization names across documents
- Mismatched signatures
- Duplicate invoices
- Reused financial statements
- Conflicting addresses
- Suspicious document metadata

These indicators do not automatically prove fraud but should trigger additional review.

---

# 10. Missing Documentation

Applications missing one or more mandatory documents should generally not proceed directly to approval.

Depending on the importance of the missing documentation, reviewers may:

- request clarification
- request resubmission
- place the application on hold
- route for manual review
- reject the application

---

# 11. Manual Review Triggers

Applications should be referred to a human reviewer when:

- Registration certificate is unreadable.
- Bank details cannot be verified.
- Organization names differ between documents.
- Proposal and budget contain conflicting figures.
- Mandatory pages are missing.
- Supporting documents appear manipulated.
- Multiple versions of the same document are submitted.
- Important information has been redacted without explanation.
- Scanned documents are incomplete.
- OCR confidence is insufficient to verify key fields.

---

# 12. Data Privacy

Applicants should avoid including unnecessary personal information.

Where beneficiary information is provided, organizations should ensure that:

- consent has been obtained where required,
- personal information is limited to what is necessary,
- confidential records are handled securely.

Reviewers should access applicant information only for grant evaluation purposes.

---

# 13. Record Retention

Application documents should be retained in accordance with organizational record retention policies.

Rejected applications may be archived for audit purposes.

Approved applications should maintain complete documentation throughout the grant lifecycle.

---

# 14. Reviewer Guidance

Documentation review is intended to establish whether sufficient evidence exists to continue the evaluation process.

Reviewers should focus on completeness, consistency, readability, and authenticity rather than the quality of the proposed project itself. Project merit, financial assessment, and funding decisions are evaluated in later stages of the review pipeline.

Applications with strong documentation should proceed to eligibility and financial review. Applications with uncertain or conflicting documentation should be routed for manual verification before a final recommendation is made.

---

# 15. Policy Revision

This policy will be reviewed annually or whenever changes in regulatory requirements, organizational procedures, or grant management practices require updates.

The latest approved version of this policy supersedes all previous documentation guidelines.
