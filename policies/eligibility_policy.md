# Eligibility Policy

**Document ID:** ELG-POL-001
**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Policy Owner:** Grant Operations Department

---

# 1. Purpose

This Eligibility Policy defines the minimum legal, operational, financial, and governance requirements that an organization must satisfy before it can be considered for grant funding.

The purpose of eligibility screening is to ensure that organizational resources are awarded only to legally recognized and operationally capable organizations that demonstrate responsible governance, regulatory compliance, and the capacity to deliver the proposed project.

Meeting all eligibility requirements allows an application to proceed to detailed review. It does not guarantee funding approval.

---

# 2. Scope

This policy applies to every grant application submitted under the organization's funding programs regardless of:

- Funding amount
- Geographic location
- Program category
- Previous funding history
- Project duration

The policy should be applied consistently to all applicants to ensure fairness, transparency, and accountability.

---

# 3. Eligible Organization Types

The following entities may apply for funding, subject to compliance with all applicable requirements:

- Registered Non-Governmental Organizations (NGOs)
- Charitable Trusts
- Registered Societies
- Section 8 Companies
- Non-profit Foundations
- Community-based Organizations recognized by government authorities
- Public charitable institutions

Organizations must be legally authorized to receive grant funding within their jurisdiction.

---

# 4. Legal Registration Requirements

Applicants must possess valid legal registration.

Minimum requirements include:

- Registration issued by a recognized government authority.
- Registration remains active on the application date.
- Registration certificate has not expired.
- Organization has not been dissolved.
- Registration details match submitted documentation.

Applications lacking verifiable legal registration shall normally be rejected.

---

# 5. Operational History

Organizations should demonstrate sufficient operational maturity.

Expected evidence includes:

- Minimum two years of organizational operations.
- Previous community development activities.
- Documented project implementation.
- Public records where available.
- Organizational reports or annual reports.

Organizations established within the previous two years may still be considered but should normally be routed for manual review.

---

# 6. Organizational Governance

Applicants should maintain an appropriate governance structure.

Expected governance practices include:

- Clearly identified governing board.
- Executive leadership.
- Defined organizational responsibilities.
- Authorized signatory.
- Internal decision-making procedures.

Organizations lacking basic governance structures may receive reduced evaluation confidence.

---

# 7. Regulatory Compliance

Applicants are expected to comply with all applicable laws and regulations.

Compliance includes:

- Registration requirements
- Tax obligations
- Labor regulations
- Financial reporting obligations
- Charity regulations
- Anti-corruption legislation
- Data protection requirements

Known regulatory violations should be investigated before funding decisions are made.

---

# 8. Financial Identity

Applicants should possess a legitimate financial identity.

Organizations should provide:

- Active organizational bank account
- Bank account registered under the organization's legal name
- Tax Identification Number (PAN or equivalent)
- Financial statements when requested

Bank account ownership should correspond to the legal entity submitting the application.

---

# 9. Authorized Representatives

Applications must be submitted by an individual authorized to represent the organization.

Authorized representatives should have authority to:

- Submit grant applications
- Accept funding agreements
- Provide supporting documentation
- Respond to clarification requests

Applications submitted without organizational authorization should not proceed.

---

# 10. Conflict of Interest

Organizations should disclose any actual, potential, or perceived conflicts of interest.

Examples include:

- Board members related to grant reviewers.
- Financial relationships with decision-makers.
- Undisclosed ownership interests.
- Personal financial benefit from funding decisions.

Disclosure of a conflict does not automatically disqualify an applicant. Undisclosed conflicts may result in rejection.

---

# 11. Previous Grant Performance

Where applicable, previous funding history may be reviewed.

Positive indicators include:

- Timely reporting
- Successful project completion
- Proper financial utilization
- Achievement of project objectives
- Positive audit findings

Negative indicators include:

- Misuse of funds
- Failure to submit reports
- Contract violations
- Repeated deadline failures
- Unresolved audit findings

Previous performance should inform reviewer confidence but should not be the sole basis for rejection.

---

# 12. Organizational Capacity

Applicants should demonstrate sufficient organizational capacity to deliver the proposed project.

Relevant factors include:

- Staff experience
- Project management capability
- Financial management systems
- Technical expertise
- Volunteer support
- Existing partnerships

Organizations proposing projects significantly larger than previous initiatives should receive additional scrutiny.

---

# 13. Beneficiary Eligibility

Projects should clearly define intended beneficiaries.

Applications should identify:

- Target population
- Geographic area
- Estimated beneficiary count
- Selection methodology
- Inclusion strategy

Projects that discriminate on prohibited grounds without lawful justification may be deemed ineligible.

---

# 14. Ethical Standards

Applicants are expected to maintain ethical organizational practices.

Organizations should demonstrate commitment to:

- Transparency
- Accountability
- Non-discrimination
- Child protection
- Gender equality
- Responsible use of public funds
- Respect for human rights

Evidence of unethical organizational practices should be investigated.

---

# 15. Ineligible Organizations

The following organizations are generally not eligible for funding:

- Organizations operating without legal registration.
- Organizations under government suspension.
- Organizations dissolved or liquidated.
- Organizations prohibited from receiving grants.
- Organizations involved in fraudulent activities.
- Organizations convicted of serious financial misconduct.
- Organizations sanctioned under applicable law.

Funding exceptions require executive approval.

---

# 16. Automatic Rejection Conditions

Applications should normally be rejected where:

- Registration cannot be verified.
- Registration has expired.
- Submitted documents are fraudulent.
- Organization identity cannot be confirmed.
- Organization is legally prohibited from receiving grants.
- Applicant intentionally provides false information.
- Mandatory legal documentation is missing.
- Organization is operating under regulatory suspension.

---

# 17. Manual Review Conditions

Applications should be referred to manual review when:

- Registration information contains minor inconsistencies.
- Organization is newly established.
- Financial identity requires verification.
- Governance documentation is incomplete.
- Additional clarification is required.
- External verification is pending.
- Supporting evidence is incomplete.
- Previous grant history cannot be confirmed.

Manual review should resolve uncertainty rather than replace automated eligibility assessment.

---

# 18. Risk Indicators

Eligibility reviewers should pay particular attention to:

- Frequent changes in organization name.
- Multiple registrations using identical addresses.
- Shared bank accounts across organizations.
- Inconsistent leadership information.
- Unusually recent registration dates.
- Significant discrepancies between proposal and organizational capacity.
- Missing governance documentation.
- Conflicting legal information across submitted documents.

Presence of one indicator does not necessarily imply misconduct. Multiple indicators should increase reviewer attention.

---

# 19. Reviewer Guidance

Eligibility review should focus on determining whether the applicant possesses the legal authority, organizational capacity, governance standards, and regulatory compliance necessary to receive grant funding.

Reviewers should avoid making judgments regarding project quality, innovation, or financial efficiency during this stage. Those aspects are evaluated separately under financial assessment and funding evaluation policies.

Where evidence is insufficient but not clearly negative, applications should be routed for manual review rather than automatically rejected.

---

# 20. Policy Maintenance

This policy shall be reviewed annually or whenever legal, regulatory, or organizational requirements change.

Revisions should ensure continued compliance with applicable laws, funding standards, and organizational governance practices.

The most recently approved version supersedes all previous editions of the Eligibility Policy.
