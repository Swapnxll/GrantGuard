# Financial Policy

**Document ID:** FIN-POL-001
**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Policy Owner:** Finance and Grant Management Department

---

# 1. Purpose

This Financial Policy establishes the standards for preparing, reviewing, approving, and monitoring budgets submitted as part of grant applications. The policy ensures that grant funds are used responsibly, transparently, and solely for activities that directly support approved project objectives.

Financial review is intended to evaluate whether a proposed budget is reasonable, complete, internally consistent, and aligned with the proposed activities. Passing financial review does not guarantee funding approval but indicates that the financial information satisfies the organization's minimum review requirements.

---

# 2. Scope

This policy applies to all grant applications submitted to the organization regardless of:

- Funding program
- Requested grant amount
- Organization size
- Geographic location
- Project duration

All applicants are expected to comply with this policy throughout the grant lifecycle, including proposal submission, implementation, reporting, and final project closure.

---

# 3. Financial Principles

All budgets submitted for review should adhere to the following principles:

- Accuracy
- Transparency
- Accountability
- Cost effectiveness
- Value for money
- Reasonable pricing
- Proper documentation
- Responsible stewardship of grant funds

Applicants are expected to demonstrate prudent financial planning and avoid unnecessary or excessive expenditures.

---

# 4. Budget Preparation Requirements

Each application must include a detailed itemized budget.

The budget should clearly identify:

- Expense category
- Description of each expense
- Quantity
- Unit cost
- Total cost
- Funding requested
- Applicant contribution, where applicable
- Justification for significant expenditures

Budget summaries without supporting line items may be routed for manual review.

---

# 5. Eligible Expense Categories

Grant funding may generally be used for expenses directly related to project implementation.

Examples include:

## Personnel

- Project managers
- Field coordinators
- Trainers
- Community workers
- Technical specialists
- Administrative staff directly supporting the project

Personnel costs should reflect prevailing market rates.

---

## Equipment

Eligible equipment may include:

- Computers
- Tablets
- Medical equipment
- Educational materials
- Project-related machinery
- Communication devices
- Safety equipment

Equipment purchases should clearly support project delivery.

---

## Training

Allowable training expenses include:

- Venue rental
- Training materials
- Facilitator fees
- Learning resources
- Participant refreshments
- Evaluation materials

Training activities should be linked to project objectives.

---

## Travel

Travel costs may include:

- Local transportation
- Field visits
- Accommodation
- Meals during official travel
- Vehicle rental where justified

International travel generally requires additional approval.

---

## Monitoring and Evaluation

Projects are encouraged to allocate funding for:

- Surveys
- Data collection
- Impact assessment
- Independent evaluation
- Reporting
- Monitoring visits

---

## Communication

Reasonable communication expenses may include:

- Internet services
- Telephone
- Printing
- Community outreach
- Awareness campaigns

---

# 6. Administrative Expenses

Administrative costs should remain reasonable and proportionate to the project size.

Examples include:

- Office utilities
- Accounting support
- Administrative salaries
- Office supplies
- Document management

Administrative expenses should generally not exceed **20%** of the total requested grant amount unless specifically justified.

Budgets exceeding this threshold should be referred for manual review.

---

# 7. Funding Thresholds

The following thresholds are intended to guide automated review.

### Small NGOs

Recommended maximum funding:

**₹5,00,000**

---

### Medium NGOs

Recommended maximum funding:

**₹15,00,000**

---

### Large NGOs

Recommended maximum funding:

**₹50,00,000**

Applications requesting amounts above these thresholds are not automatically rejected but require additional financial justification and manual review.

---

# 8. Budget Consistency

The proposed budget should be consistent with:

- Project duration
- Timeline
- Number of beneficiaries
- Scope of work
- Organizational capacity
- Expected project outcomes

For example, a project proposing to serve fifty beneficiaries should not include expenses appropriate for a nationwide initiative without adequate justification.

Significant inconsistencies reduce reviewer confidence.

---

# 9. Cost Reasonableness

Applicants should ensure that estimated costs reflect realistic market prices.

Reviewers may consider:

- Historical project costs
- Comparable market rates
- Vendor quotations
- Public pricing information
- Regional cost variations

Excessive pricing should be explained within the proposal.

---

# 10. Procurement Standards

Organizations are expected to follow fair procurement practices.

Recommended practices include:

- Competitive quotations
- Transparent vendor selection
- Avoidance of conflicts of interest
- Proper documentation
- Written purchase records
- Receipt retention

Large procurements should be supported by multiple quotations whenever practical.

---

# 11. Ineligible Expenses

Grant funds must not be used for activities unrelated to approved project objectives.

Examples include:

- Personal luxury items
- Alcoholic beverages
- Gambling activities
- Political campaigning
- Religious conversion activities
- Personal loans
- Debt repayment unrelated to the project
- Personal gifts
- Fines or legal penalties
- Speculative investments
- Cryptocurrency trading
- Entertainment unrelated to project delivery

Budgets containing prohibited expenses may be rejected or routed for manual review depending on severity.

---

# 12. Financial Documentation

Applicants should maintain adequate financial records throughout project implementation.

Expected documentation includes:

- Invoices
- Receipts
- Contracts
- Payroll records
- Bank statements
- Procurement documentation
- Vendor quotations
- Payment confirmations

Organizations may be required to provide these records during audits or monitoring visits.

---

# 13. Financial Reporting

Funded organizations should submit periodic financial reports that include:

- Budget utilization
- Variance explanations
- Remaining balances
- Major procurements
- Supporting documentation
- Forecasted expenditures

Late or incomplete reporting may affect eligibility for future funding.

---

# 14. Fraud Indicators

Financial reviewers should remain alert for potential indicators of financial misconduct.

Examples include:

- Duplicate invoices
- Round-number budgets without justification
- Identical quotations from multiple vendors
- Unrealistic salary allocations
- Repeated miscellaneous expenses
- Inflated procurement costs
- Missing calculations
- Mathematical inconsistencies
- Unexplained budget revisions
- Conflicting financial documents

These indicators should increase reviewer attention but do not independently establish fraud.

---

# 15. Manual Review Triggers

Applications should be referred for manual financial review when:

- Requested funding exceeds recommended thresholds.
- Administrative costs exceed 20%.
- Significant mathematical errors are detected.
- Major budget categories lack justification.
- Equipment purchases appear excessive.
- Procurement plans are unclear.
- Financial records are incomplete.
- Budget totals differ between documents.
- Project costs appear inconsistent with organizational capacity.

---

# 16. Automatic Rejection Conditions

Financial review may recommend rejection where:

- Financial information is intentionally falsified.
- Fraudulent invoices are submitted.
- Budget is primarily composed of prohibited expenses.
- Significant financial misrepresentation is identified.
- Applicant refuses to clarify major discrepancies.
- Required financial documentation is intentionally withheld.

Where uncertainty exists, manual review should be preferred over automatic rejection.

---

# 17. Reviewer Guidance

Financial review should evaluate whether proposed expenditures are reasonable, justified, and aligned with project objectives.

Reviewers should avoid evaluating project impact or organizational eligibility during this stage, as those responsibilities belong to other components of the grant review process.

Financial reviewers should document significant observations, identify supporting evidence, and clearly distinguish between confirmed policy violations and issues requiring clarification.

---

# 18. Record Retention and Audit

Organizations receiving funding are expected to retain financial records for a minimum period specified by applicable regulations or grant agreements.

The organization reserves the right to conduct:

- Financial audits
- Compliance reviews
- Procurement inspections
- Documentation verification
- Site visits

Applicants should cooperate fully with audit activities.

---

# 19. Policy Maintenance

This policy shall be reviewed annually or whenever changes in financial regulations, accounting standards, procurement practices, or organizational grant management procedures require revision.

The latest approved version replaces all previous editions of the Financial Policy.
