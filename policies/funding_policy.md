# Funding Policy

**Document ID:** FUND-POL-001
**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Policy Owner:** Grant Programs and Funding Committee

---

# 1. Purpose

This Funding Policy establishes the principles, priorities, evaluation criteria, approval framework, and funding conditions governing all grant programs administered by the organization.

The objective of this policy is to ensure that grant funding is allocated fairly, transparently, and consistently to projects that demonstrate meaningful social impact, responsible financial planning, and the organizational capacity to deliver measurable outcomes.

Funding decisions should balance community needs, organizational capability, financial responsibility, and long-term sustainability.

Compliance with this policy supports evidence-based funding decisions but does not replace the judgment of the Grant Review Committee.

---

# 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- Competitive grant programs
- Community development grants
- Education initiatives
- Healthcare initiatives
- Environmental projects
- Disaster relief programs
- Women empowerment initiatives
- Skill development programs
- Digital inclusion projects

All applicants are evaluated under the same policy regardless of funding amount or geographic region.

---

# 3. Funding Objectives

The organization seeks to support projects that produce measurable and sustainable improvements within communities.

Funding priorities include:

- Improving access to education
- Strengthening public health
- Supporting women and girls
- Promoting environmental sustainability
- Enhancing livelihoods
- Building community resilience
- Supporting marginalized communities
- Encouraging innovation in social development
- Expanding digital accessibility
- Promoting disaster preparedness and recovery

Projects should clearly demonstrate how proposed activities contribute to one or more funding objectives.

---

# 4. Guiding Principles

Funding decisions should be guided by the following principles:

- Fairness
- Transparency
- Accountability
- Community impact
- Equity
- Sustainability
- Responsible stewardship of funds
- Evidence-based decision making
- Ethical conduct
- Non-discrimination

These principles should inform both automated and manual reviews.

---

# 5. Project Eligibility

Projects should:

- Address a clearly defined community need.
- Present realistic objectives.
- Include measurable outcomes.
- Demonstrate implementation capacity.
- Align with at least one funding priority.
- Present a feasible implementation timeline.
- Include a reasonable budget.

Projects lacking clear objectives may receive lower evaluation scores.

Preference may be given to projects operating in the following sectors.

- Digital learning

# 7. Innovation

Innovative projects are encouraged when they:

- Introduce new approaches.
- Demonstrate scalability.
- Improve efficiency.
- Reduce implementation costs.
- Increase beneficiary reach.
- Generate measurable outcomes.

Innovation alone should not outweigh project feasibility.

---

# 8. Sustainability

Applicants should explain how project benefits will continue after grant funding ends.

Strong sustainability plans may include:

- Community ownership
- Local partnerships
- Revenue generation
- Volunteer engagement
- Government collaboration
- Capacity building

Projects that rely entirely on continued grant funding may receive lower evaluation scores.

---

# 9. Partnerships

Collaboration with other organizations is encouraged.

Potential partners include:

- Government agencies
- Educational institutions
- Healthcare providers
- Local community groups
- International organizations
- Private sector organizations

Applicants should clearly describe partner responsibilities.

---

# 10. Beneficiary Impact

Applications should clearly describe:

- Target beneficiaries
- Estimated beneficiary count
- Expected outcomes
- Geographic coverage
- Community engagement strategy

Reviewers should assess whether expected outcomes are realistic and supported by the proposed implementation plan.

---

# 11. Monitoring and Evaluation

Every funded project should define measurable indicators.

Examples include:

- Number of beneficiaries served
- Training completion rates
- Employment outcomes
- Health improvements
- Educational performance
- Environmental indicators
- Infrastructure completed

Applicants should also describe:

- Data collection methods
- Reporting frequency
- Performance monitoring
- Risk mitigation strategies

---

# 12. Funding Decisions

Applications generally fall into one of four categories.

## Approved

Application demonstrates:

- Strong documentation
- Policy compliance
- Financial feasibility
- Organizational capacity
- Acceptable risk

---

## Approved with Conditions

Funding may proceed after specific conditions are satisfied.

Examples include:

- Minor budget revisions
- Additional documentation
- Timeline clarification
- Procurement clarification

---

## Manual Review

Applications requiring further assessment include:

- Conflicting evidence
- Policy ambiguity
- Large funding requests
- Complex partnerships
- Newly established organizations
- Insufficient supporting evidence

---

## Rejected

Applications may be rejected due to:

- Ineligibility
- Fraud
- Major policy violations
- Financial misconduct
- Significant documentation deficiencies
- Prohibited activities

---

# 13. Funding Conditions

Successful applicants are expected to:

- Sign a funding agreement.
- Use funds only for approved activities.
- Maintain accurate financial records.
- Submit progress reports.
- Cooperate with monitoring activities.
- Permit financial audits where required.
- Notify the organization of significant project changes.

Failure to comply may result in suspension or recovery of grant funds.

---

# 14. Project Amendments

Material changes require prior approval.

Examples include:

- Budget reallocations
- Timeline extensions
- Change in project objectives
- Change in implementation location
- Change in key personnel
- Major procurement changes

Unauthorized amendments may affect continued funding.

---

# 15. Risk Considerations

Funding decisions should consider:

- Financial risk
- Operational risk
- Reputational risk
- Compliance risk
- Delivery risk
- Procurement risk
- Governance risk

Projects presenting elevated risk are not automatically rejected but may require additional oversight or phased funding.

---

# 16. Ethical Standards

Applicants are expected to uphold high standards of integrity throughout the grant lifecycle.

Organizations should:

- Prevent corruption.
- Avoid conflicts of interest.
- Protect beneficiary data.
- Respect human rights.
- Promote inclusion and non-discrimination.
- Report misuse of funds.
- Maintain transparent governance.

Violations of ethical standards may result in suspension or termination of funding.

---

# 17. Reviewer Guidance

Funding review should consider the application holistically. Reviewers should evaluate the strength of the proposal, anticipated social impact, financial realism, implementation feasibility, and consistency with organizational priorities.

Automated review outputs should be treated as decision support rather than final determinations. Where evidence is incomplete or conflicting, reviewers should recommend manual assessment instead of making unsupported approval or rejection decisions.

Funding recommendations should be supported by documented evidence drawn from organizational policies, submitted application materials, and the results of specialized review tools.

---

# 18. Policy Maintenance

This policy shall be reviewed annually or whenever strategic priorities, regulatory requirements, or organizational funding practices change.

The Grant Programs Committee is responsible for approving revisions and ensuring that all reviewers use the latest approved version.

The current version supersedes all previous editions of the Funding Policy.
